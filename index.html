Chapter#1

سورة الفاتحة

Reciting Surah Al-Fatiha, the opening chapter of the Quran, holds numerous benefits for Muslims. Here are some of the significant benefits associated with reciting Surah Al-Fatiha:


1. Essential Obligation:


Surah Al-Fatiha is a fundamental part of the obligatory Muslim prayer (Salah). It is recited in every unit of the prayer, emphasizing its importance and significance in daily worship.

2.Spiritual Connection:


Reciting Surah Al-Fatiha establishes a direct connection between the individual and Allah. It serves as a means to communicate with the Almighty, seeking guidance, blessings, and mercy.

3.Opening Supplication:


Al-Fatiha is often referred to as the "Opening" because it serves as an introduction to the Quran. It lays the foundation for the recitation of the Quran and opens the doors for a profound understanding and reflection on its teachings.

4.Praise and Gratitude:


The Surah begins with praising Allah, acknowledging His sovereignty, mercy, and the countless blessings bestowed upon humanity. By reciting Surah Al-Fatiha, Muslims express their gratitude to Allah for His benevolence.

5.Seeking Guidance:


Surah Al-Fatiha contains a supplication for guidance, wherein Muslims seek Allah's help and wisdom in navigating their lives and making righteous decisions. It acts as a reminder that seeking divine guidance is crucial for leading a purposeful and righteous life.

6.Remission of Sins:


Reciting Surah Al-Fatiha with sincerity and understanding can lead to the forgiveness of sins. It serves as a reminder of seeking repentance from past transgressions and starting anew on a path of righteousness.

7.Emotional Healing:


The recitation of Surah Al-Fatiha has a soothing effect on the heart and mind. It provides solace, tranquility, and comfort, helping individuals find peace in times of distress, grief, or anxiety.

8.Reflecting on Divine Attributes:


Surah Al-Fatiha highlights some of Allah's attributes, such as mercy, sovereignty, and judgment. Reciting this Surah regularly encourages contemplation and reflection upon these divine qualities, fostering a deeper understanding of Allah's nature.

9.Unity in Worship:


Surah Al-Fatiha is recited collectively in congregational prayers, emphasizing the unity of the Muslim community. It serves as a reminder that Muslims, regardless of their backgrounds, come together in worship, reinforcing a sense of unity, brotherhood, and equality.

10.Spiritual Rewards:


Reciting Surah Al-Fatiha and engaging with the Quranic verses with sincerity and devotion earns spiritual rewards from Allah. These rewards are believed to be manifold, offering blessings and benefits in both this world and the Hereafter.


It is important to note that the benefits of reciting Surah Al-Fatiha go beyond this list. The Quran is a profound source of guidance, and engaging with its verses, including Surah Al-Fatiha, brings numerous blessings and spiritual benefits to those who recite and contemplate upon them.

Chapter#2

سورة البقرة

Reciting Surah Al-Baqarah holds great significance in Islam, and it is believed to have numerous benefits. Here are some of the reported benefits of reciting Surah Al-Baqarah:


1.Protection against Shaytan (Satan):


It is believed that reciting Surah Al-Baqarah acts as a means of protection against Shaytan and his whispers. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said that Satan does not enter a house in which Surah Al-Baqarah is recited.

2.Blessings and Barakah:


Reciting Surah Al-Baqarah is said to bring blessings and barakah (divine blessings) into one's life, home, and wealth. It is believed to increase one's sustenance and bring prosperity.

3.Protection for the Household:


Surah Al-Baqarah is considered a means of protection for the household. Reciting it regularly is believed to ward off evil, protect against harm, and create a peaceful and harmonious environment.

4.Forgiveness of Sins:


Reciting Surah Al-Baqarah is believed to have the potential to forgive sins. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said that whoever recites Surah Al-Baqarah and believes in it, his past sins will be forgiven.

5.Intercession on the Day of Judgment:


It is believed that those who regularly recite Surah Al-Baqarah will be granted intercession on the Day of Judgment. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said that the Quran will come on the Day of Judgment as an intercessor for those who used to recite it.

6.Reward and Blessings:


Each letter recited from Surah Al-Baqarah is said to carry immense rewards. The longer the recitation, the greater the blessings. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said that whoever recites the two opening verses of Surah Al-Baqarah will be granted blessings until the morning.


It is important to note that these benefits are based on religious beliefs and traditions, and their extent and nature may vary among different individuals and communities. The ultimate reward and benefit of reciting the Quran, including Surah Al-Baqarah, lies in seeking the pleasure of Allah and striving to follow its guidance in all aspects of life.

Prophet's صلى الله عليه وسلم Sunnahs

1. Praying Five Daily Salah:

Performing the obligatory prayers (Salah) regularly and on time.

2. Using Siwak (Miswak):

Cleaning the teeth with a siwak/miswak, a natural tooth-cleaning twig.

3. Using the Right Hand:

Eating, drinking, and giving/receiving items with the right hand.

4. Beginning with Bismillah:

Saying "Bismillah" (In the name of Allah) before starting any action.

5. Saying "Alhamdulillah":

Expressing gratitude to Allah by saying "Alhamdulillah" (All praise is due to Allah).

6. Saying "Masha'Allah" and "TabarakAllah":

Saying "Masha'Allah" (Allah has willed) and "TabarakAllah" (Blessed is Allah) when admiring something or someone

7. Saying "SubhanAllah," "Alhamdulillah," and "Allahu Akbar":

Engaging in dhikr (remembrance) by saying "SubhanAllah" (Glory be to Allah), "Alhamdulillah" (All praise is due to Allah), and "Allahu Akbar" (Allah is the Greatest).

8. Using the Siwak Before Praying:

Using the siwak before performing the prayers.

9. Praying the Sunnah Prayers:

Voluntary prayers offered before or after the obligatory prayers.

10. Saying "SadaqAllahul Azim":

Saying "SadaqAllahul Azim" (Allah, the Almighty, has spoken the truth) after reciting the Quran in public.

11. Offering Duha Prayer:

A voluntary prayer performed after the sun has fully risen

12. Sleeping on the Right Side:

Sleeping on the right side as the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) did.

13. Reciting Ayat al-Kursi:

Reciting Ayat al-Kursi (Quran, Surah Al-Baqarah, 2:255) before sleeping.

14. Saying the Dua for Entering and Exiting the House:

Saying the respective duas when entering and leaving the house.

Dua for Entering the House:

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ وَلَجْنَا، وَبِسْمِ اللَّهِ خَرَجْنَا، وَعَلَى رَبِّنَا تَوَكَّلْنَا

Transliteration: Bismillahi walajna, wa bismillahi kharajna, wa 'alaa Rabbina tawakkalna. Translation: In the name of Allah, we enter, and in the name of Allah, we leave, and upon our Lord, we rely.


Dua for Leaving the House:

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ تَوَكَّلْتُ عَلَى اللَّهِ، وَلَا حَوْلَ وَلَا قُوَّةَ إِلَّا بِاللَّهِ

Transliteration: Bismillahi tawakkaltu 'alallah, wa la hawla wa la quwwata illa billah. Translation: In the name of Allah, I place my trust in Allah; there is no power and no strength except with Allah.

15. Making Dua (Supplications):

Making personal supplications after the obligatory prayers, as it is a time when dua is more likely to be accepted.

16. Greeting with "Salam":

Greeting others with "Assalamu Alaikum" (Peace be upon you) is a Sunnah and an act of kindness.

17. Responding to "Salam":

Responding to the Islamic greeting with "Wa Alaikum Assalam" (And upon you be peace).

18. Saying "Bismillah" before Eating or Drinking:

Saying "Bismillah" before starting to eat or drink.

19. Saying "Alhamdulillah" after Sneezing:

Saying "Alhamdulillah" (All praise is due to Allah) after sneezing, and responding with "Yarhamukallah" (May Allah have mercy on you) to the person who sneezed.

20. Reciting Specific Surahs in Prayer:

Reciting particular Surahs (chapters) in specific prayers, such as Surah Al-Kafirun and Surah Al-Ikhlas in the two Rak'ahs before Fajr prayer.

21. Raising Hands during Takbirs:

Raising hands when saying "Allahu Akbar" in the opening Takbir of prayer and when moving from one posture to another.

22. Saying "SubhanAllah" after Burping:

Saying "SubhanAllah" (Glory be to Allah) after burping.

23. Saying "La ilaha illallah" when in Distress:

Saying "La ilaha illallah" (There is no god but Allah) during times of distress or difficulty.

24. Entering and Leaving the Restroom with the Left Foot:

Entering the restroom with the left foot and leaving with the right foot.

25. Reciting the Last Two Verses of Surah Al-Baqarah at Night:

Reciting the last two verses of Surah Al-Baqarah before sleeping; they are said to bring blessings and protection.

26. Giving Charity Secretly:

Giving charity (Sadaqah) secretly, without showing off, to maintain sincerity.

27. Helping Others:

Helping those in need and assisting others in various ways.

28. Maintaining Good Character:

Emulating the Prophet's (peace be upon him) excellent character by being kind, patient, and respectful.

29. Refraining from Arguing and Using Foul Language:

Avoiding arguments and using foul language, even when provoked.

30. Reciting Specific Duas in Different Situations:

earning and reciting various supplications for specific situations, such as before sleeping, waking up, traveling, or entering the home.

31. Visiting the Sick:

Visiting and offering prayers for those who are sick or in distress.

32. Maintaining Family Ties:

Fostering good relationships with relatives and maintaining family ties.

Reciting Surah Al-Mulk (Quran, Chapter 67) before going to sleep, as it is said to protect from the punishment of the grave

Performing the Witr prayer, which is a voluntary prayer offered after the Isha prayer.

Offering two voluntary Rak'ahs before the Fajr prayer.

Whispering the call to prayer (Adhan) in the right ear of a newborn baby shortly after birth.

Whispering the Iqamah (shortened call to start the prayer) in the left ear of a newborn baby shortly after birth.

Making personal supplications between the Adhan and Iqamah during congregational prayers.
